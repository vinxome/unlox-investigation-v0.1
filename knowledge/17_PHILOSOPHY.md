# Scope

This document extracts philosophical foundations already established
elsewhere in the investigation. It introduces no new principle,
assumption, abstraction, or example. Every section below traces to a
specific point of origin, noted in italics. Where something was
discussed but not clearly concluded, it is marked as a TODO rather
than included as settled.

This file is distinct from 18_GOVERNING_PRINCIPLES.md (not yet
drafted): that file will hold specific stable rules (e.g. "Opportunity
is operationally primary," "Fit is preferred over exhaustive search").
This file holds the underlying worldview those rules follow from.

# Diagnosis and Design Must Never Be Mixed

The foundational separation of the entire methodology: understanding
current reality is a different activity from changing it, and every
element of the investigation must belong to one or the other, never
both at once. Structural Realities, UDEs, Objectives, Needs, Clouds,
and Assumptions all describe existing reality. Injections, Negative
Branch Reservations, the Future Reality Tree, and Product all describe
intended change. Nothing may describe both.

*Origin: Systems Investigation Methodology v1, the founding document —
"This methodology separates understanding reality from changing
reality... Diagnosis and design must never be mixed."*

**Corollary, stated in the same document:** "Every element of the
methodology must describe either existing reality, or intended change.
Never both."

This principle was not merely stated once and left behind — it was
tested and re-applied repeatedly throughout the investigation whenever
diagnostic wording was found to smuggle in solution-shaped language
(for example: an Assumption reworded because "no perceived mechanism"
read as a claim about missing capability rather than an observation;
a UDE reworded because it asserted unobservable motivation rather than
behavior; the Ecosystem Constraint's Dominant Structural Cause reworded
because "no verifiable, portable way to represent trust" named a
candidate Injection in advance rather than describing the current
blocked state). Each of these was a specific instance of this same
foundational principle, not a separate rule.

# No Claim of Completeness

The investigation does not permit any stakeholder, UDE, Cloud, or
finding to be described as fully resolved or exhaustively validated —
only as the current best model given the evidence gathered so far.

*Origin: methodology validation exercise (Task 4), stated explicitly
while confirming three independent stakeholder investigations had
converged on comparable structures — the validation itself was
described as provisional, not as proof of completeness.*

This is why retired Wall items are preserved with a stated reason
rather than deleted, why flagged uncertainties (e.g. the demand-side
propagation inference, the C4 compensation-allocation fork) are
recorded as open rather than resolved by assertion, and why stakeholder
coverage gaps (six of nine stakeholders not yet investigated) are
stated plainly rather than implied to be unimportant.

# Stakeholder-First: Boundarylessness Belongs to the Investigator

Each stakeholder experiences the ecosystem differently, and the
methodology exists to preserve those distinct perspectives before
attempting to reconcile them into a single view. Where the
investigator sees connections across stakeholders, the stakeholders
themselves do not necessarily share that view — the ecosystem-level
picture is the investigator's construction, not something any single
participant experiences directly.

*Origin: the instruction that reorganized the UDE Wall from a
topic-first to a stakeholder-first structure — "The investigation
should respect stakeholder boundaries during observation.
Boundarylessness belongs to the investigator, not to the stakeholders.
Each stakeholder experiences the ecosystem differently. The
methodology must preserve those perspectives before attempting to
reconcile them."*

This is also the basis for building stakeholder-level CRTs
independently before assembling any ecosystem-level view, and for
treating cross-stakeholder interaction as a distinct, later
investigation stage rather than something to infer while a single
stakeholder's CRT is still being built.

# The Wall Is Canonical; the CRT Is a View

The UDE Wall holds the raw, first-person observational record. A CRT
is an analytical view derived from it, optimized for reasoning rather
than for storage. Provenance must remain available but must not
interrupt the reading of the causal argument itself — which is why
traceability lives separately from the diagram (see
19_TRACEABILITY.md, not yet filed) rather than embedded inline within
every node.

*Origin: the discussion that established three distinct views of the
investigation — Wall (everything, every source), CRT (only the causal
chain, readable), and Traceability (click a node, see its sources) —
concluding "the Wall is canonical... let the CRT optimize for
thinking, not for storage."*

---

# CRTs Are Meant to Be Read and Argued With, Not Decoded

A synthesized or compressed node that states a causal conclusion
without showing the steps that produce it functions as an analyst's
summary, not as something a reader can independently follow or
challenge. Every CRT in this investigation shows its causal chain
explicitly — each step is either a direct statement from the Wall or
an explicitly marked inferred connection — rather than compressing
several observations into one abstracted node.

*Origin: the review that rejected an earlier CRT pilot's synthesized
"Trunk UDE" nodes as hiding reasoning rather than exposing it, and
called for the diagram to read "like a CRT... not like a compression
algorithm," closer to how Goldratt's own CRTs were meant to be read,
challenged, and discussed.*

# Local Reasoning Before Global Reasoning

Each stakeholder's Current Reality is investigated and owned
independently first. Only where those independent realities actually
interact — evidenced by a genuine cross-boundary causal link, not
merely proximity or thematic similarity — does an arrow cross between
them. Most causal structure is expected to remain local to a single
stakeholder; only specific, evidenced interactions cross a boundary.

*Origin: the "box architecture" / CRT-of-CRTs discussion, which
described this as encapsulation applied to stakeholder ownership —
"Each stakeholder owns their current reality. Only where realities
interact do arrows cross the box" — and was adopted with two explicit
conditions: a cross-boundary arrow is not automatically evidence of a
Cloud, and the hypothesis that most structure stays local was treated
as something to verify empirically rather than assume.*

This principle is why Internal → Intra-Stakeholder → Inter-Stakeholder
was investigated strictly in that order for every stakeholder pair
examined, and why an empty finding (no evidenced interaction — as with
Buyer↔Buyer) was recorded as a genuine result rather than treated as
an incomplete investigation.

# Respecting Existing Needs When Designing Change

An Assumption is a participant's belief about why a particular Need is
currently considered necessary to achieve an Objective. Different
assumptions can sustain different, individually legitimate Needs. When
two such Needs become incompatible under the assumptions currently
sustaining them, a Cloud is diagnosed. It follows that an eventual
Injection does not remove or override a legitimate Objective or Need —
it challenges one or more of the assumptions sustaining the Cloud, so
that a path satisfying both Needs becomes possible.

*Origin: explicit agreement, without identified inconsistency, that
this describes the relationship between Assumptions, Needs, and
Injections correctly — reached during discussion of how Assumptions
relate to the Needs they sustain.*

---

# Ontological Parsimony: Verify Before Positing

Before introducing a new entity or a new shared coordinating identity
to explain an observed effect, first verify whether an existing
participant already privately possesses the relevant fact, and the
actual gap is only a missing event — not a missing entity.

*Origin: the resolution of whether Property needed to function as an
operational anchor in the ontology. The evidence (a stale listing,
discovered weeks after a sale) was fully explained by a Seller already
possessing knowledge of their own closed deal and that knowledge
failing to propagate to their other active relationships — not by any
participant needing a shared, coordinated identity for the property
itself. The broader rule was extracted directly from this specific
correction, not stated independently of it.*

---

# Three Separated Domains

The investigation treats Objective Reality, Operational Ontology, and
Computational Implementation as three distinct domains that must not
be collapsed into one another. In particular, implementation concepts
must not be allowed to determine ontology — a canonical
information-architecture assumption (for example, that any persistent
entity requires a unique identity every representation resolves
through) is not itself evidence that the ecosystem's operational model
requires the same structure.

*Origin: stated directly in the investigation status record, and
demonstrated in practice during the same Property-as-anchor
resolution above — the original "anchor" framing was diagnosed as
having crossed from the domain of physical/legal reality into the
domain of computational/informational architecture without evidence
licensing that crossing.*
