# Why These Remain in 3-Box Form

All three Clouds below are Objective → Need → Cloud, not the full
5-box Objective → Need → Want → Cloud form used for the Ecosystem
Cloud (see CCC.md). This is a deliberate, explained choice, not an
oversight:

Compression from 5-box to 3-box is adequate for diagnosis. It stops
being adequate the moment a Cloud is used to derive or shape an
Injection — because an Injection targets an Assumption, and whether
that Assumption sits at the true-Need level (a genuine, irreducible
trade-off) or the Want level (a coordination problem masking
compatible Needs) determines what kind of Injection is correct. No
stakeholder-specific Injection has been derived from any Cloud below,
independent of the shared Ecosystem Injection — so this distinction
has not yet cost anything real. If one ever is derived independently
for a single stakeholder, that Cloud needs the same 5-box unpacking
the Ecosystem Cloud received, first.

**Related, unresolved tension (see 14_NEEDS.md TODO #1):** on
inspection, all three Needs pairs below are already strategy-shaped —
closer to what a Want would look like than a fundamental Need. This
was noted during a later ontology-adjacent discussion and never
applied back to these frozen CRTs. Flagged here again for visibility,
not resolved.

# Broker's Internal Cloud

**Objective:** Maintain a thriving brokerage practice. (full text: 13_OBJECTIVES.md)
**Need 1:** Protect my client relationships.
**Need 2:** Increase my reach. (full text: 14_NEEDS.md)

**Cloud:** Protect my relationships ⟷ Increase my reach.

**Statement (verbatim from frozen diagram):** "Both paths lead to the
same place: portal dependence and a weaker position than I started
with. Protecting relationships starves my reach; chasing reach hands
control of that reach to a platform. Neither need can be pursued
without undermining the other."

**Assumptions (implicit in the chain, not separately unpacked —
see 3-box note above):**
- A1: Sharing or systematizing client relationships and track record
  beyond private records means losing what differentiates the broker
  and losing control over clients and commissions.
- A2: The only ways to reach further than a personal network are
  depending on a platform (which controls visibility and outranks the
  broker's own brand) or personally sustaining more relationships than
  is realistic.

# Seller's Internal Cloud

**Objective:** Sell my property for a good outcome, with confidence
in the process.
**Need 1:** Enough market exposure to attract the best possible buyers
and price.
**Need 2:** Trust that whoever represents me is genuinely committed to
my sale.

**Cloud:** Enough market exposure ⟷ One accountable, trusted broker.

**Statement (verbatim from frozen diagram):** "Doubt about a single
broker's adequacy pushes me toward appointing more brokers. But once
multiple brokers are competing for the same commission, none is
individually accountable — so reach comes at the direct cost of
trust, and staying with one broker leaves the original exposure doubt
unresolved and unverifiable either way."

**Structural note:** this Cloud is cyclical, not two static branches
meeting at the bottom — the doubt underlying Need 2 is itself what
drives action toward Need 1 (appointing more brokers), and acting on
Need 1 is exactly what undermines Need 2. This was an explicit finding
when the CRT was built, not a simplification made here.

**Assumptions (implicit in the chain):**
- A1: One broker alone might not reach enough of the market —
  appointing more brokers is the only way to increase the chances of
  being seen.
- A2: When multiple brokers compete for the same commission, none has
  strong enough individual incentive to fully commit — more brokers
  means less accountability, not more.

# Buyer's Internal Cloud

**Objective:** Find and buy a property that's right for me, with
confidence in the price and the process.
**Need 1:** See everything available, so I don't miss the right
property.
**Need 2:** One coherent, trustworthy source guiding me through the
whole process.

**Cloud:** See everything available ⟷ One trustworthy, coordinated guide.

**Statement (verbatim from frozen diagram):** "Searching broadly to
avoid missing anything is exactly what produces the inconsistency and
noise that stops me from confidently settling on one guide. Settling
on one guide risks missing what exists outside their view. Neither
path resolves the anxiety."

**Assumptions (implicit in the chain):**
- A1: The only way to make sure of not missing the right property is
  to check as many different sources as possible.
- A2: Settling on one broker/source to trust and coordinate everything
  risks missing properties or deals that exist outside what that one
  source shows.
