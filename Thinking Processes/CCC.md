# Why This Cloud Is Intentionally 5-Box

Unlike the three stakeholder Clouds (CLOUDS.md, kept in 3-box form),
this Cloud is fully unpacked: Objective → Need B / Need C → Want D /
Want D' → Cloud. This is because the Ecosystem Cloud is the one
Cloud in the investigation actually used to inform Injection
derivation — and at that point, compression stops being adequate. An
Injection targets an Assumption; getting the Assumption's real
location right (Want-level, not Need-level) determines whether the
resulting Injection is correct.

This was not the Cloud's original form. It went through two corrections:

1. **The original draft stated the conflict at the Need level** —
   "broad reach" versus "narrow, verified accountability." This was
   reviewed and rejected: nowhere in the investigation was maximum or
   broad reach ever actually argued for by any stakeholder. Every
   stakeholder Need, checked directly against wall evidence, asked for
   *qualified* or *relevant* reach, not maximum exposure (Buyer's own
   U012 — "the listings I see first aren't the best fit, just the ones
   someone paid to put on top" — is a direct complaint about
   volume-driven discovery, not a request for more of it).
2. **The Need-level framing was replaced with the current 5-box
   form.** Need B (qualified reach) and Need C (trusted representation)
   do **not** conflict with each other. The conflict lives entirely at
   the Want level: the only currently-available tactic for each Need
   (broaden exposure and filter after, for B; keep representation
   narrow and exclusive, for C) happens to directly undermine the
   other.

# The Cloud

**Objective:** Reliably convert existing qualified supply and demand
into qualified opportunities. (full text: 13_OBJECTIVES.md)

**Need B:** Qualified reach — the right supply must reach the right
demand, not necessarily the widest possible audience.

**Need C:** Trusted representation — effort and claims must be
attributable to a specific, verifiable, accountable party.

**Want D (serves Need B):** Broaden exposure as widely as possible,
then filter for relevance afterward.

**Want D' (serves Need C):** Keep representation narrow and exclusive.

**Cloud (verbatim from frozen diagram):** "Needs B and C are NOT in
conflict — both are blocked by the same dominant structural cause.
The conflict lives entirely at the Want/strategy level: the only
currently-available tactic for each Need happens to directly undermine
the other. Broadening exposure (D) exposes information to parties
outside the current trusted relationship, destroying the exclusivity
D' depends on; narrowing to protect accountability (D') caps the reach
D requires."

**Dominant Structural Cause:** trust, effort, and market knowledge
exist only in personal memory — no one outside that relationship can
observe, check, or rely on them. This is not the Constraint itself
(see CRT.md, Ecosystem CRT section) and is not itself marked as
current-state — it is the validated diagnostic finding explaining why
Want D and Want D' are currently the only available tactics.

# Assumption Analysis (summary — full detail in 15_PREREQUISITES.md)

Every assumption sustaining Want D, Want D', and the conflict between
them was traced to a merged, irreducible set of three:

| # | Assumption | Classification | Supports |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | No third party can observe, verify, or rely on another party's trust, effort, track record, or claims without direct personal experience | Structural (Technological facet) | Both D and D' |
| 2 | Compensation for a closed transaction goes entirely to one party, not distributed by contribution | Institutional | D' primarily, threatens D |
| 3 | Meaningful accountability requires a single responsible party — shared responsibility collapses to none | Behavioural | D' only |

Everything else considered during the analysis (demand-blindness,
probability-chasing behavior, stigma toward stale listings, trust
non-transferability, information non-containment) was found to be a
derived restatement of assumption #1, not independent of it.
**Assumption #2 carries an explicitly unresolved fork** — it is not
settled whether it reduces further to #1 or is an independent
institutional convention. See 15_PREREQUISITES.md for the full
evidence trail (portal-aggregation, brokerage-franchise, and
high-ticket-KYC evidence tests) and the complete, unmerged assumption
list (B1–B4, C1–C4, X) that produced this table.

# Relationship to the Stakeholder Clouds

Broker's, Seller's, and Buyer's own Internal Clouds (CLOUDS.md) are
each marked, in the frozen Ecosystem CRT, as an **instance of** this
Cloud's Want-level conflict — not the Need level. This is consistent
with the observation (14_NEEDS.md TODO #1) that stakeholder-level
Needs are themselves already Want-shaped: Broker's "protect
relationships vs. increase reach," Seller's "exposure vs. one broker,"
and Buyer's "see everything vs. one guide" are strategy-level
conflicts, the same as Want D vs. Want D' here, not fundamental-need
conflicts. This Cloud was derived independently of the three
stakeholder Clouds and converged on the same shape without
cross-reference during construction — treated in the original
investigation as a strong validation, not a coincidence to explain away.
